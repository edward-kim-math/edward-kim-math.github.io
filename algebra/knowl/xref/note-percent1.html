Note 1.8.9.

It is extremely important to point out that the original expression in part 2 is \(21.50 - 0.20 \times 21.50\text{,}\) which is where all of the other answers (including \(17.20\)) come from. The specific thing to pay attention to is that the original price of \(21.50\) dollars appears twice in the first expression. This actually makes sense: we would have paid \(21.50\) dollars if there was no discount, but because of the discount, we are paying less (and the amount that we are paying less is proportional to the original price, and so it makes sense that the amount we are subtracting also involves the original price of \(21.50\) dollars).
🔗
As an example of Principle 1.8.1, we are taking \(20\%\) percent of \(21.50\text{.}\)
