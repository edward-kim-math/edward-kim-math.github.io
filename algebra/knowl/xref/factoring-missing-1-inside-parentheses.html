Warning 1.1.78.

When factoring \(9x + 9\) it is common to make the mistake of saying that this expression is equal to \(9(x)\) instead of \(9(x + 1)\text{.}\) When we see \(9x+9\text{,}\) to successfully factor out a \(9\) we will write an expression in the format \(9(? + ?)\text{,}\) and the first question mark is filled in by asking “9 times what is the first term \(9x\)”, while the second question mark is filled in by asking “9 times what is the second term \(9\)”.
🔗
To provide a little more convincing, you can always check your factoring by taking your answer and distributing. Note that distributing in the expression \(9(x+1)\) gives us \(9x+9\text{.}\)
