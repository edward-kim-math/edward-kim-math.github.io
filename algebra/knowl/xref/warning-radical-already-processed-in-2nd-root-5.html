Warning 1.5.55.

It would be incorrect to write
\begin{equation*} \sqrt[2]{25} = \sqrt[2]{5}. \end{equation*}
Of course, the completion to the right of the equal sign should have a \(5\text{,}\) so when someone writes \(\sqrt[2]{5}\text{,}\) it is good to see the \(5\) present, since they are thinking about the correct base. However, the radical should not surround the \(5\) because we have already processed what the radical does.
