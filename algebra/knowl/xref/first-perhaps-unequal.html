Example 1.1.24.

Based only on the Order of Operations, are \((x-y) \cdot z\) and \(x - y \cdot z\) equal or do we not have enough information?
🔗
Answer.
  • In the first expression \((x-y) \cdot z\text{,}\) we perform the subtraction \(x - y\) first because the subtraction is in parentheses. Then we perform the multiplication of the result and \(z\) to get \((x - y) \cdot z\text{.}\)
    🔗
  • In the second expression \(x - y \cdot z\text{,}\) there are no parentheses. We perform the multiplication \(y \cdot z\) first. Then we perform the subtraction of \(x\) and \(y \cdot z\) to get \(x - y \cdot z\text{.}\)
    🔗
In the first expression, we perform the subtraction first, and then the multiplication next. In the second expression, we perform the multiplication first, and then the subtraction next. Based only on the Order of Operations, we do not have enough information to determine if the two expressions are equal. (These expressions might be equal or they might not, but we cannot determine this based only on the Order of Operations. If these expressions happened to be equal, this would have to be explained by something other than the Order of Operations.)
