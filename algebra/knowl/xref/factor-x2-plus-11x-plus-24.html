Example 1.6.42.

Factor \(x^2+11x+24\text{.}\)
🔗
Answer.
Because we have a quadratic trinomial with positive constant term \(24\) and we are being asked to factor, Strategy 1.6.41 applies. We are looking for two numbers to fill in the blanks in
\begin{equation*} (x+\_\_\_)(x+\_\_\_). \end{equation*}
Examining the constant term \(24\) and the linear coefficient \(11\text{,}\) the two blanks must be filled with positive numbers that multiply to \(24\) and add to \(11\text{.}\) To do this, we can list pairs of numbers whose product is \(24\text{:}\)
  • \(1\) and \(24\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(2\) and \(12\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(3\) and \(8\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(4\) and \(6\)
    🔗
    🔗
Then, look through the list to see which pair of numbers adds up to \(11\text{.}\) These are \(3\) and \(8\text{,}\) in either order, so we can factor \(x^2+11x+24\) as
\begin{equation*} (x+3)(x+8)\text{.} \end{equation*}
