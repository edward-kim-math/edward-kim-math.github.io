Strategy 1.6.116.

To factor \(Ax^2+Bx+C\text{,}\) draw the following diagram: The remaining two spaces (on the left and right) need to be filled with two numbers that multiply to the number \(AC\) at the top of the diamond, and add to \(B\) at the bottom of the diamond. It is often helpful to write all the factor pairs of \(AC\text{,}\) and then go through the list to see which pair of numbers adds to \(B\text{,}\) using the signs of \(AC\) and \(B\) to help narrow down the possibilities. Once the two numbers are found, multiply each by \(x\text{,}\) and then consider the sum of these together with \(Ax^2\) and \(C\text{.}\) This will consist of four terms, so we can apply Strategy 1.6.102 to factor the expression completely.
