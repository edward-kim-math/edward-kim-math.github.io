Principle 1.1.74. Factoring Produces Factors.

The process of factoring produces factors. Recall from Definition 1.1.32 that a factor is a piece of a product. Even in the example we just did, we ended with the expression \(4(c+d)\) which is equal to the original expression \(4c+4d\text{.}\) In the new expression, \(4\) is a factor and \((c+d)\) is a factor, since these are the pieces of the multiplication in the product \(4 \cdot (c+d)\text{.}\)
🔗
In short, factoring creates factors.
