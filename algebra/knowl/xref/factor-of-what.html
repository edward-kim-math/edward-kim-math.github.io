Note 1.1.39.

When we say an expressions is a factor, we should be clear what it is a factor of.
  • \(z\) is a factor of the product \(y \cdot z\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(z\) is a not factor of the entire expression \(x - y \cdot z\)
    🔗
    🔗
🔗
For clarification, in the last example which had \(x - y \cdot z\text{,}\) we would say that \(z\) is a factor of the product \(y \cdot z\) which came from part of the expression, but we would not say that \(z\) is a factor of the entire expression \(x - y \cdot z\text{.}\)
🔗
Similarly (and using an expression of the same format), \(2\) is not a factor of \(x - 4 \cdot 2\text{,}\) but \(2\) is a factor of \(4 \cdot 2\text{.}\)
