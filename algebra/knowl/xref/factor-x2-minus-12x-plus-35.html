Example 1.6.44.

Factor \(x^2-12x+35\text{.}\)
🔗
Answer.
Because we have a quadratic trinomial with positive constant term \(35\) and we are being asked to factor, Strategy 1.6.41 applies. We are looking for two numbers to fill in the blanks in
\begin{equation*} (x-\_\_\_)(x-\_\_\_). \end{equation*}
Examining the constant term \(35\) and the linear coefficient \(-12\text{,}\) the two blanks must be filled with positive numbers that multiply to \(35\) and add to \(12\text{.}\) (We are looking at \(12\) because the absolute value of \(-12\) is \(12\text{.}\)) We list the pairs of numbers whose product is \(35\text{:}\)
  • \(1\) and \(35\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • \(5\) and \(7\)
    🔗
    🔗
Then, look through the list to see which pair of numbers adds up to \(12\text{.}\) These are \(5\) and \(7\text{,}\) in either order, so we can factor \(x^2-12x+35\) as
\begin{equation*} (x-5)(x-7)\text{.} \end{equation*}
