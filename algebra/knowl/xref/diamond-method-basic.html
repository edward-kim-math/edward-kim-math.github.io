Strategy 1.6.47.

To factor \(x^2+Bx+C\text{,}\) draw the following diagram: The remaining two spaces (on the left and right) need to be filled with two numbers that multiply to the number \(C\) at the top of the diamond, and add to \(B\) at the bottom of the diamond. It is often helpful to write all the factor pairs of \(C\text{,}\) and then go through the list to see which pair of numbers adds to \(B\text{.}\) In Strategy 1.6.41, any minus signs were accounted in the structure by writing \((x+\_\_\_)(x+\_\_\_)\) or \((x-\_\_\_)(x-\_\_\_)\) thus the previous technique only had has think about positive numbers to fill in the blanks, but in this technique, the numbers themselves that are written in the left and the right of the diamond figure may be negative.
