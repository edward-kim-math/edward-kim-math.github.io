Logical operations

{% highlight latex %} Negation: $\neg p$ Conjunction: $p \wedge q$ Disjunction: $p \vee q$ Implication: $p \rightarrow q$ Biconditional: $p \leftrightarrow q$ {% endhighlight %}

Truth table

{% highlight latex %} \begin{center} \begin{tabular}{c|c||c} $p$ & $q$ & $p \wedge q$ \\ \hline $T$ & $T$ & $T$ \\ $T$ & $F$ & $F$ \\ $F$ & $T$ & $F$ \\ $F$ & $F$ & $F$ \end{tabular} \end{center} {% endhighlight %} The version above has $ surrounding each T or F value, but this may be too tedious. Feel free to instead leave out the $s for Ts and Fs as in the following example: {% highlight latex linenos %} \begin{center} \begin{tabular}{c|c||c} $p$ & $q$ & $p \rightarrow q$ \\ \hline T & T & T \\ T & F & F \\ F & T & T \\ F & F & T \end{tabular} \end{center} {% endhighlight %} In the { } after \begin{tabular}, you see c for centered text, followed by | for drawing a vertical line, a c for centered text, || for double vertical line (use just one vertical line if you don't like the look of the double vertical line), followed again by c for centering. The double-backslash gets a new row of the table, while \hline creates a horizontal line.

How do I get alignment of mathematical notation?

Use the & symbol to get alignment to happen. Below is a sample bit of code: {% highlight latex %} \begin{align*} a &= b \\ &= c \\ &\leq d \end{align*} {% endhighlight %} You can copy-paste the above into your code to see that the first line will be a=b, the next line has =c and the last line will be "less than or equal to d". Because the & is right in front of equal signs and the \leq, it is these three symbols (one per line) which will be aligned vertically.
